In the second half of the year, the increase in Membership Rewards expense was also driven by an increase in the Ultimate Redemption Rate (URR) and slightly higher redemption costs reflecting a shift in the mix of Card Member redemptions. The Membership Rewards URR for current program participants was 96 percent (rounded down) at both December 31, 2024 and 2023. Business development expense increased, primarily due to increased partner payments driven by higher network volumes, partially offset by lower client incentives and a prior-year charge related to revenue allocated to a joint venture partner. Card Member services expense increased, primarily due to growth in premium card accounts, contributing to a higher usage of travel-related benefits. Marketing expense increased, reflecting higher levels of spending on customer acquisition and other growth initiatives. Salaries and employee benefits expense increased, primarily driven by higher incentive and compensation costs, partially offset by lower restructuring costs.
