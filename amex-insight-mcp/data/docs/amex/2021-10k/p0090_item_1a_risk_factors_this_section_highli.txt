Joint ventures, including our joint venture in China, and minority investments in companies such as American Express Global Business Travel (GBT) inherently involve a lesser degree of control over business operations, thereby potentially increasing the financial, legal, operational and/or compliance risks associated with the joint venture or minority investment, including as a result of being subject to different laws or regulations. In addition, we may be dependent on joint venture partners, controlling shareholders or management who may have business interests, strategies or goals that are inconsistent with ours. GBT has entered into a business combination agreement with a third party, the consummation of which will result in GBT becoming a public company. GBT currently plans to continue to conduct its day-to-day business under its existing name and brand pursuant to a license from us even after it becomes a public company. Business decisions or other actions or omissions of a joint venture partner, other shareholders or management of our joint ventures and companies in which we have minority investments may adversely affect the value of our investment, result in litigation or regulatory action against us and otherwise damage our reputation and brand.
